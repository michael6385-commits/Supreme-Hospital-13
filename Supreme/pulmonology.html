COPD

Pneumonia

Treatment for lung tissue infections caused by bacteria, viruses, or fungi.

Pulmonary Fibrosis

Condition characterized by scarring of lung tissue affecting breathing.

Sleep Apnea

Treating disorders where breathing is interrupted during sleep.

Precision Assessment

Diagnostic & Treatment Tools

Pulmonologists use various diagnostic tools and procedures to assess respiratory health. These may include pulmonary function tests (PFTs), chest X-rays, CT scans, bronchoscopy, and sleep studies.

Depending on the condition, treatment may include medications, inhalers, oxygen therapy, pulmonary rehabilitation, lifestyle changes (such as smoking cessation), and in some cases, surgery.

"Early diagnosis and appropriate management can significantly improve the quality of life for individuals with respiratory problems."

Health Guidance

Frequently Asked Questions

A pulmonologist specializes in diagnosing and treating conditions related to the lungs and respiratory system. Common issues include asthma, chronic bronchitis, COPD, pneumonia, lung infections, sleep apnea, and interstitial lung diseases.

During your first visit, the pulmonologist will review your medical history, ask about symptoms, and perform a physical examination.

Pulmonary rehabilitation is a supervised program that combines exercise, education, and breathing strategies to improve lung function and quality of life.

Smoking damages lung tissues, narrows airways, and increases the risk of chronic respiratory diseases and lung cancer. Quitting at any stage brings health benefits—lung function improves, inflammation reduces, and the risk of serious illness declines. It’s never too late to quit and start healing your lungs.
