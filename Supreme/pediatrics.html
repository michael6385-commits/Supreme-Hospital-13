Specialized Approach

How Does Paediatrics Differ?

As a leading Pediatric Hospital, our treatment for children is different from adult medicine in more ways than one. The smaller body of an infant or neonate or a child is substantially different physiologically from that of an adult.

Treating children is not like treating a miniature adult. Congenital defects, genetic variance, and developmental issues are of greater concern to pediatricians than physicians treating adults.

In addition, the issues of guardianship, privacy, legal responsibility and informed consent are carefully considered in every pediatric procedure.

Parental Guidance

Frequently Asked Questions

Monitor your child's growth using standard growth charts that track height, weight, and body mass index (BMI). Regular pediatric check-ups help assess development and address concerns promptly. Remember, children grow at their own pace, but consistent monitoring ensures they're on a healthy trajectory.

Encourage a positive mealtime environment by offering a variety of foods without pressuring your child. Involve them in meal preparation to spark interest. Serve the same meals for the whole family and avoid making separate dishes.

Schedule your child's first dental visit after their first tooth appears, but no later than their first birthday. Early dental visits help prevent cavities and establish good oral hygiene habits from the start.

If you notice your child isn't reaching expected developmental milestones, consult your pediatrician promptly. Early intervention is crucial, as timely support can help your child catch up and reach their full potential.
