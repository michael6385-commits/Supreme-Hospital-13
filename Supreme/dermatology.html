Frequently Asked Questions

Dermatologists treat skin, hair, and nail conditions such as acne, eczema, psoriasis, fungal infections, skin allergies, and skin cancer. They also manage cosmetic concerns like pigmentation, wrinkles, and scars. Early consultation helps in effective diagnosis and treatment of both medical and cosmetic dermatological issues.

Treatments for acne scars include chemical peels, microneedling, laser therapy, dermal fillers, and subcision. The best option depends on scar type and skin condition. A dermatologist can assess your skin and recommend a personalized treatment plan to improve texture and appearance over time.

Yes, cosmetic dermatology procedures are generally safe when performed by qualified professionals. Treatments like Botox, fillers, and laser therapy are FDA-approved and carry minimal risks. It's important to consult a certified dermatologist to ensure procedures are suitable for your skin type and medical history.

Consult a dermatologist if you notice sudden, excessive, or patchy hair loss, thinning, or scalp irritation. Early diagnosis can help identify the underlying cause—whether hormonal, nutritional, or medical—and allow timely treatment to prevent further hair loss and promote regrowth.
